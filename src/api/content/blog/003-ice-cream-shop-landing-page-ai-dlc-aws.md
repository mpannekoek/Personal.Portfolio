# From Local Scoop to Live Site

Recently, I built a new landing page for a local ice cream shop in our village.  
The goal was simple: make it easy for visitors to quickly understand what the shop offers, where to find it, and why it is worth a visit.

This was not a big enterprise project with dozens of stakeholders. It was a practical, local business case where speed, clarity, and trust mattered most.

## Project Goal

The landing page needed to do three things well:

1. Show the shop's personality and local vibe.
2. Highlight key visitor information (opening times, location, menu highlights).
3. Turn visitors into action by making directions and contact easy from mobile.

In projects like this, the challenge is not only writing code. It is making the right product decisions with limited time and still shipping something polished.

## How AI-DLC on AWS Helped

I used AI-DLC on AWS as a practical accelerator during development.  
It helped me iterate faster on structure, content direction, and implementation choices while I stayed in control of final decisions.

The biggest value was in shortening the feedback loop:

- quickly test variants for messaging and section order
- validate copy tone for a local audience
- speed up implementation details without sacrificing quality

For a small business landing page, this kind of support is valuable because the project budget and timeline are usually tight.

## Build Decisions

I focused on a few decisions that had the most impact:

- **Clear first screen**: immediate message, local context, and primary call-to-action.
- **Mobile-first layout**: most visitors will discover the page on their phone.
- **Fast loading**: lightweight assets and simple content structure.
- **Practical content hierarchy**: essentials first, extra details later.

I also kept the page easy to maintain so future updates can be made quickly as seasons, flavors, or opening times change.

## Outcome So Far

The new page now gives the business a cleaner online presence and a stronger first impression for new visitors.  
It is easier to scan, easier to navigate, and better aligned with what people actually need when they are deciding where to go.
